Belfast Murals

Political & Cultural Street Art

About Belfast Murals

Belfast's murals are among the most famous examples of political and cultural street art in the world. These powerful artworks tell the story of Belfast's complex history, from the Troubles to the peace process and beyond, serving as both historical documents and calls for peace.

The murals are primarily located in loyalist and republican areas, with the most famous being along the Falls Road (republican) and Shankill Road (loyalist). The Peace Walls, which still separate some communities, feature many of these artworks and have become a symbol of Belfast's divided past and hopeful future.

Today, many murals have evolved from purely political statements to celebrations of culture, history, and international solidarity. They provide visitors with a unique insight into Belfast's transformation from a city in conflict to one embracing peace and reconciliation.

Things to Do

Black Taxi Tour

Take a traditional black taxi tour with local drivers who provide personal insights into the history and meaning behind the murals.

Peace Walls

Visit the Peace Walls that separate communities and see how they've been transformed into canvases for messages of hope and reconciliation.

Walking Tours

Join guided walking tours that explore the historical context and artistic significance of the murals in different neighborhoods.
