Old Bushmills Distillery

World's Oldest Licensed Whiskey Distillery

About Old Bushmills Distillery

The Old Bushmills Distillery, established in 1608, holds the distinction of being the world's oldest licensed whiskey distillery. Located in the charming village of Bushmills in County Antrim, it has been producing premium Irish whiskey for over 400 years, making it a cornerstone of Irish whiskey heritage.

The distillery is renowned for its smooth, triple-distilled Irish whiskey, which is made using traditional methods passed down through generations. The pure water from Saint Columb's Rill, which flows through the nearby Giant's Causeway, is a key ingredient that gives Bushmills its distinctive character.

Today, visitors can explore the working distillery, learn about the whiskey-making process, and sample some of the world's finest Irish whiskey. The distillery offers guided tours that take you through each step of production, from mashing and fermentation to distillation and maturation.
